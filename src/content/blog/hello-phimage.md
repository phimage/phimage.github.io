## Why "Phimage", and that logo

Read the name out loud and it splits in two: **Phi · mage**. A little mage of *phi* — φ,
the golden ratio.

That's the whole logo, if you look:

![The Phimage logo: a gold golden-rectangle bracket and an orange phi, together forming a P](/logo.png)

- the **gold bracket** is a golden rectangle, left open
- the **orange φ** sits inside it — a circle with a bar through, the golden number itself
- put together, the two shapes read as a **P** — for Phimage

A φ and a rectangle, separated the way the golden ratio divides a line, arranged so the
negative space spells the first letter of the name. That's it. I like things that mean
one more thing than they first appear to.
